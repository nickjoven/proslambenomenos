Thesis

In ancient Greek music theory, the proslambenomenos (προσλαμβανόμενος) was the lowest pitch in the Greater Perfect System — the reference from which every interval was measured. It did not belong to any tetrachord. It simply was.

This repository proposes a physical analogue: the cosmological constant $\Lambda$ sets a fundamental oscillation frequency, and the MOND acceleration scale, the Hubble rate, and the galactic synchronization threshold are all overtones of this single reference. They are not independent coincidences — they are intervals measured from the proslambenomenos.

$\Lambda$, $H_0$, and $a_0$ are not three independent measurements. They are one frequency measured in three different units.

The Chain: $\Lambda \to H_0 \to a_0$

The cosmological constant sets the vacuum's fundamental oscillation frequency:

$$\nu_\Lambda \;=\; c\,\sqrt{\Lambda/3} \;\approx\; 5.7 \times 10^{-18}\;\text{s}^{-1} \;\approx\; H_0$$

The MOND acceleration threshold is the product of this frequency and the coupling propagation speed, divided by $2\pi$ (the Kuramoto critical coupling normalization):

$$a_0 \;=\; \frac{c\,\nu_\Lambda}{2\pi} \;=\; \frac{c\,H_0}{2\pi} \;\approx\; 1.1 \times 10^{-10}\;\text{m\,s}^{-2}$$
$\Lambda$  →  $c\sqrt{\cdot/3}$  →  $\nu_\Lambda \approx H_0$  →  $c/2\pi$  →  $a_0$
curvature → frequency → acceleration
Three constants. One frequency. Zero free parameters.

Why $2\pi$?

The $2\pi$ is not a fudge factor. It is the ratio of angular frequency to cycle frequency — the same $2\pi$ that appears in the Kuramoto critical coupling formula $K_c = 2/(\pi g(0))$. The proslambenomenos is a cycle frequency; the locking window is measured in angular frequency.

Lyapunov Functional and Uniqueness

The Renzo's Rule derivation in 201 left uniqueness as an open problem after PR #7 replaced the contraction mapping strategy with a Lyapunov approach. This repository closes that gap.

Uniqueness is not a mathematical property of the equilibrium — it is a physical property of the path.

The functional

The Kuramoto model (finite, identical frequencies) admits:

$$V[\theta] = -\frac{K}{2N}\sum_{i,j}\cos(\theta_i - \theta_j), \qquad \frac{dV}{dt} = -\sum_i \dot{\theta}_i^2 \leq 0$$

The continuum extension under the Kuramoto–Einstein mapping becomes:

$$\mathcal{V}_{\text{KE}}[\gamma, \psi] = -\frac{1}{2}\iint G_\gamma(x,x')\cos[\psi(x') - \psi(x)]\sqrt{\gamma}\,\sqrt{\gamma'}\,d^3x\,d^3x'$$

In the synchronized limit, this reduces to the gravitational potential energy functional — the Lyapunov function of Newtonian gravity is the synchronized limit of the Kuramoto–Einstein Lyapunov function.

The dissipation argument

Phase 1 — Incoherent: $r \ll 1$. Coupling subcritical everywhere. Full MOND regime. $\mathcal{V}_{\text{KE}} \approx 0$.

Phase 2 — Nucleation: Synchronization nucleates at the dense core. Coherent patches expand outward. $\mathcal{V}_{\text{KE}}$ falls.

Phase 3 — Relaxation: System reaches unique fixed point. Rotation curve determined. $\mathcal{V}_{\text{KE}}$ at minimum.

The fixed point is unique because the path is dissipative. Even if the equilibrium equations admit multiple solutions, only one is reachable from physically reasonable initial conditions (desynchronized, approximately homogeneous) via monotone Lyapunov descent.

Renzo's Rule from Kuramoto Self-Consistency

The ADM-side derivation in 201 proves Renzo's Rule from the Hamiltonian constraint. This repository provides the independent Kuramoto-side proof.

The mechanism

At the synchronization fixed point, the self-consistency equation is:

$$r(x) = \int K(x,x')\,\rho_b(x')\,P_{\text{lock}}[\rho_b(x'), r(x')]\,\cos\Delta\psi\;d^3x'$$

The baryonic density $\rho_b$ appears directly in the integrand because:

$$\omega(x) = \sqrt{4\pi G\,\rho_b(x)}$$

The baryonic distribution is the frequency distribution. Every spatial feature in $\rho_b$ modifies the locking condition, which modifies $r(x)$, which modifies the rotation curve.

Forward and inverse

Forward A localized perturbation $\delta\rho_b$ at radius $R_0$ produces $\delta v^2$ at $R_0$ through the linearized self-consistency equation. Baryonic features mirror in the rotation curve.

Inverse The phantom density $\rho_{\text{phantom}} = \rho_{\text{crit}}(1 - r)$ is a convolution through the Green's function kernel — a low-pass filter. It cannot produce features sharper than those in $\rho_b$. Sharp rotation curve features must have baryonic origins.

The Sheet Music

The Ott–Antonsen reduction gives a 1D flow on the coherence order parameter:

$$\dot{r} = \gamma\,r\,\bigl[x(1 - r^2) - 1\bigr], \qquad x = a/a_0$$

The phase portrait

A phase portrait in $(r, \dot{r})$ space, parameterized by the local acceleration ratio $x = a/a_0$:

  • $x \gg 1$ (Newtonian): Stable fixed point at $r^* = \sqrt{1 - 1/x} \approx 1$. Full synchronization. Stars locked to the vacuum frequency.
  • $x = 1$ (MOND boundary): The fixed point collides with $r = 0$. Transcritical bifurcation.
  • $x \ll 1$ (deep MOND): The fixed point vanishes ($r^* = 0$). Coherence drifts. The "stick" regime.

Sweeping $x$ from $10$ down to $0.05$ animates the transition from Newtonian gravity to MOND — the same transition a star experiences as it orbits from the dense core to the diffuse outskirts of a galaxy.

The Ott–Antonsen potential

The potential landscape $U(r) = \frac{\gamma}{2}r^2 - \frac{K}{4}r^2 + \frac{K}{8}r^4$ whose minimum is the fixed point. For $x > 1$, a well traps the system in synchronization. At $x = 1$, the well vanishes — the MOND boundary.
